Green supply chain management is about incorporating environmental considerations into supply chain management, which includes product design, material sourcing and selection, manufacturing processes, and final product delivery to customers, as well as product end-of-life management. Initially, the move toward environmental sustainability was largely driven by increasing pressure on businesses from various stakeholders such as government regulators, community activists, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), and consumers, but evidence suggests that green supply chain management can also help companies reduce costs and increase revenues.
